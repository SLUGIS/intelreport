State of the Weather

923 AM PST MON FEB 2 2015
A RIDGE OF HIGH PRESSURE WILL BUILD ACROSS SOUTHWEST CALIFORNIA
THROUGH WEDNESDAY WITH DRY CONDITIONS CONTINUING ACROSS THE FIRE
DISTRICT. THE MARINE INVERSION WILL BE SHALLOW TODAY BUT DEEPENS
SLIGHTLY THE NEXT FEW DAYS BRINGING THE POTENTIAL FOR LATE NIGHT AND
MORNING LOW CLOUDS AND FOG TO COASTAL AREAS. TEMPERATURES SHOULD
REMAIN NEAR OR JUST ABOVE NORMAL DURING THE WEEK WITH MOST AREAS
COOLING A FEW DEGREES AFTER TUESDAY. MINIMUM HUMIDITIES WILL BE
LOWEST IN THE MOUNTAINS TODAY AND TUESDAY WITH TEENS AND ISOLATED
SINGLE DIGIT VALUES...THEN A SLOW INCREASE IN HUMIDITIES BEYOND
WEDNESDAY AS ONSHORE FLOW INCREASES. WINDS WILL REMAIN RELATIVELY
LIGHT DURING THE WEEK EXCEPT FOR LOCALLY GUSTY WEST WINDS AT HIGHER
ELEVATIONS AND ACROSS THE ANTELOPE VALLEY EACH AFTERNOON AND EARLY
EVENING.
THE HIGH PRESSURE RIDGE WILL BREAK DOWN AND SHIFT EASTWARD BY
THURSDAY AND FRIDAY AS A STORM SYSTEM MOVES INTO NORTHERN AREAS OF
THE STATE. INCREASING HUMIDITY AND COOLER TEMPERATURES WILL BE THE
RULE DISTRICT-WIDE AND THERE WILL BE A SLIGHT RISK OF RAIN FROM
SANTA BARBARA COUNTY NORTHWARD LATE FRIDAY OR SATURDAY.
